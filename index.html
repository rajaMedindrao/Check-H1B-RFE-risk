Under the hood

How the score is built.

The calculator maps your petition inputs to the exact risk signals USCIS adjudicators look for — weighted by how often each triggers an RFE in practice.

01

You answer 18 targeted questions

Every question covers a documented RFE trigger: SOC code risk, wage-level alignment, job description specificity, degree-to-duty match, worksite structure, and filing history. Nothing superfluous.

02

Each answer maps to a weighted risk score

Inputs are scored across five categories — Specialty Occupation (30 pts), Wage Level (20), Degree Alignment (20), Worksite (15), and Employer History (15) — for a 0–100 composite. Scores update in real time as you answer.

03

Compound risks are caught automatically

Single flags are straightforward — but the model also detects dangerous combinations: Level 1 wage + 6+ years of experience, a generic JD paired with a high-scrutiny SOC code, or a first-time petitioner placing a beneficiary at a third-party client site.

04

Every flag comes with a specific fix

No vague warnings. Each flagged issue surfaces plain-English remediation — revised duty language, evidence bundles USCIS expects, or a recommendation to consult Alma counsel before filing. The goal is a petition that clears the risk threshold, not just a high score.
